How to draw Realism


Foundation of Realism Drawing:

  • Understand the Two Pillars [00:00:13]: The ability to draw realistically comes down to mastering two core skills: **Location (Proportion)** and **Value (Shading)**. Talent only dictates the speed of learning.

  • Master Location/Proportion [00:02:42]: This skill is about accurately placing lines and boundaries to form shapes on the page [00:02:50]. Knowing where every part of the drawing goes is key. Techniques that offer more freedom are generally better than more restrictive ones, such as the basic grid method [00:06:08].

  • Master Value/Shading [00:03:12]: Value controls the illusion of three-dimensional forms on a two-dimensional paper [00:03:29]. If you can get the value (light and dark) correct, you can paint.

  • Practice with Fundamentals [00:06:38]: There is no shortcut for determining the correct value. The best way to improve is to practice consistently, keeping the correct fundamental understanding in mind [00:06:46].
